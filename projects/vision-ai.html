Vision AI – Smart Industrial Operations

AI-powered computer vision for industrial safety and operations — enabling real-time anomaly detection, HSE compliance monitoring, and automated safety enforcement across existing CCTV infrastructure.

Role
Innovation Lead
Status
In Progress
Environment
Industrial Manufacturing
The Problem

Manual Monitoring Creates Blind Spots

Manual monitoring of industrial operations leads to safety risks, operational inefficiencies, and a lack of real-time insight. Human operators cannot continuously watch all CCTV feeds simultaneously — HSE violations often go undetected until after incidents occur, and equipment anomalies like belt issues are only discovered after causing downtime or material loss.

Traditional rule-based systems lack the intelligence to detect subtle patterns, contextual violations, and early-stage anomalies that indicate developing problems.

Use Cases

Two Primary Domains

Equipment Monitoring

Belt Monitoring

AI models continuously analyze conveyor belt camera feeds to detect anomalies before they cause failures or production losses.

  • Detection of belt misalignment, tears, and surface damage
  • Material spillage and carryback detection
  • Automated alerts to maintenance teams before downtime occurs
  • Historical pattern analysis to predict failure windows
Safety Compliance

HSE Monitoring

Computer vision models trained to detect health, safety, and environment violations in real time across industrial zones.

  • PPE compliance detection — helmets, vests, footwear across zones
  • Unauthorized zone entry and restricted area monitoring
  • Real-time violation alerts to safety personnel
  • Incident logging and compliance reporting
Approach

Innovation Lead Scope

Engaged as Innovation Lead to define, scope, and drive the adoption of AI-powered computer vision use cases — working across existing industrial CCTV infrastructure. The focus was on building the business case, selecting appropriate CV model approaches, and aligning the initiative with enterprise AI governance structures.

The initiative sits within the broader Enterprise AI Strategy and Transformation Leadership programme, alongside the AI steering committee and division structure established to govern AI adoption across the enterprise.
