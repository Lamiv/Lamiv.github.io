Problem

Manual monitoring of industrial operations leads to safety risks, operational inefficiencies, and a lack of real-time insights. Human operators cannot continuously watch all CCTV feeds simultaneously, HSE violations often go undetected until after incidents occur, and equipment anomalies like belt issues are only discovered after causing downtime or material loss. Traditional rule-based systems lack the intelligence to detect subtle patterns and contextual violations.

Solution

Engaged as Innovation Lead to define, scope, and drive the adoption of AI-powered computer vision use cases — working across existing industrial CCTV infrastructure to enable continuous intelligent monitoring across two primary domains:

⚙️ Belt Monitoring

AI models continuously analyze conveyor belt camera feeds to detect anomalies before they cause failures:

  • → Detection of belt misalignment, tears, and surface damage
  • → Material spillage and carryback detection
  • → Automated alerts to maintenance teams before downtime occurs
  • → Historical pattern analysis to predict failure windows

🦺 HSE Monitoring

Computer vision models trained to detect health, safety, and environment violations in real time:

  • → PPE compliance detection (helmets, vests, gloves, goggles)
  • → Unsafe behavior identification (restricted zone entry, unsafe posture)
  • → Real-time violation alerts for safety officers
  • → Incident logging and analytics for compliance reporting

Impact

💡 ROI-Driven Transformation Approach

These initiatives are being driven with a clear ROI and materiality lens — not as technology experiments, but as investments with quantifiable returns. Each use case is scoped to generate savings that exceed its implementation cost, with a path toward self-funding through reduced downtime, lower incident costs, and improved compliance. This is the model I apply to all innovation-led transformation work: ground every AI initiative in a business case before committing to build.

  • ✓ Belt downtime prevention — each unplanned stoppage carries significant material and production loss; early detection directly reduces this cost
  • ✓ HSE incident reduction — lower incident rates reduce insurance, liability, and regulatory costs while protecting workforce wellbeing
  • ✓ Zero additional hardware cost — leverages existing CCTV infrastructure, keeping deployment investment minimal
  • ✓ Enables real-time corrective action rather than post-incident response — shifting operations from reactive to proactive
  • ✓ Strengthens regulatory compliance posture, reducing exposure to audit findings and penalties
