Context

The Problem

A large continuous manufacturing enterprise was operating across three distinct areas of dysfunction: HR ran on paper-based and legacy workflows with no self-service; customer ordering was handled entirely by phone with no digital channel; and plant maintenance operated on a failed SAP implementation — using overly complex GUI transactions, opaque user statuses, and no structured phase control across the maintenance lifecycle.

Plant maintenance in particular had accumulated years of workarounds. Planners worked in transactions like IW31 and IW41 that were difficult to use, hard to audit, and disconnected from the mobile workforce. There was no clear view of where a maintenance request or order stood, no governance over workflow transitions, and no mechanism to prevent execution before safety conditions were met.

Transformation Programs

Three Transformation Programs

HR TransformationSAP SuccessFactors

HR Transformation and Digitization

Led the HR digital transformation, replacing paper-based and legacy HR processes with integrated SAP SuccessFactors workflows across the full employee lifecycle — from onboarding to performance and payroll.

  • SAP SuccessFactors implementation — core HR, onboarding, and performance management
  • Digitization of HR records, approvals, and employee self-service workflows
  • Integration with payroll and attendance management systems
  • HR analytics dashboards surfacing workforce metrics and compliance data
EcommerceSAP S/4HANABTPCPI

Ecommerce Platform × SAP S/4HANA Integration

Designed and architected the integration between a new ecommerce platform and SAP S/4HANA using modern integration patterns via SAP BTP and CPI. Built around the operational realities of manufacturing — not just generic ecommerce.

  • Capacity-driven order acceptance — orders accepted or queued based on live production capacity, not just stock
  • Multi-SKU and packaging type support with real-time S/4HANA inventory resolution
  • Automated order confirmation, delivery scheduling, and SAP posting via CPI integration flows
  • Customer-facing visibility into order status and delivery tracking
Plant MaintenanceSAP PMSAP FioriPhase-Based Maintenance

Plant Maintenance Transformation

Worked closely with the plant maintenance team to recover and redesign a failed SAP PM implementation. The existing system relied on opaque user statuses, complex GUI transactions, and disconnected mobile workflows — leaving planners with no clear visibility and technicians working around the system rather than through it.

The solution introduced a structured, phase-based maintenance model — replacing the old flat status approach with a two-level hierarchy of Phases and Sub-phases that maps directly to how maintenance work actually progresses, from request through planning, safety clearance, execution, and closure.

  • Phase Control Codes govern transitions between phases — preventing execution until prerequisites like safety permits are obtained, and enforcing structured handovers between Gatekeepers, Planners, Technicians, and Controllers
  • Fiori-based role dashboards replaced complex GUI transactions like IW31 and IW41, reducing planning cycle times by up to 25% and improving field usability for maintenance technicians
  • Maintenance Planning Buckets allow planners to group work by time period or asset set, making backlog management and material and labor availability checks significantly more structured
  • Shift from reactive to proactive maintenance — the phase model provides the governance and visibility needed to plan ahead rather than respond to breakdowns
Outcomes

Business Impact

The three programs collectively replaced the majority of manual, paper-based, and workaround-driven operations with governed, integrated digital workflows. HR processes became self-service and auditable. Customer ordering moved from phone-based to a capacity-aware digital channel. Plant maintenance shifted from an opaque, transaction-heavy system to a structured phase model with role-based Fiori applications and phase governance.

Across all three programs, the central outcome was a shift in operational control — processes that previously required constant manual coordination became trackable, governable, and visible to the right people at the right time.
