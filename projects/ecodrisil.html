The Problem

ESG Data is Fragmented and Hard to Act On

Organizations face mounting regulatory pressure to produce accurate, timely ESG reports — but ESG data is notoriously fragmented across spreadsheets, PDFs, operational systems, and third-party databases. Manual collection is slow and error-prone, carbon accounting is complex across Scope 1, 2, and 3 boundaries, and sustainability teams lack the tools to interact with their own data conversationally.

The result is delayed disclosures, compliance risk, and strategic blind spots around sustainability performance.

My Contribution

AI Architecture Advisory

Engaged as Strategic AI Advisor on a pro-bono basis, I shaped the AI architecture and product intelligence of Ecodrisil's platform. My focus was on the ESGAI® Copilot layer — the conversational AI, document intelligence, and automated reporting capabilities that differentiate the platform from traditional ESG software.

I worked with the product team to ensure the AI layer was grounded in real ESG frameworks (GRI, DEFRA) and enterprise data realities — not just technically capable, but strategically useful.
